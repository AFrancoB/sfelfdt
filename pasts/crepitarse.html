Sus cumbres están tapadas y quizás llueva. ¿Te imaginas el mar cubriendo otra vez este pedrerío papá?”
Raúl Zurita
Crackling in the Boundless Time is a string quartet that explores tension and struggle. A solid static temporal structures collides with chance, uncertainty, agency and sonic imagination. It is inspired in the poem Zurita by the poet of the same name. The visions found in this major poem, of destroyed cities and crowds turned into ashes, form the present tense of Latin-american subjectivity and the immidiate future of the global north.
